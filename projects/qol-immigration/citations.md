## Referenced studies and papers

### Immigration and labor markets

18. **Card, David.** "Immigrant Inflows, Native Outflows, and the Local Labor
    Market Impacts of Higher Immigration." *Journal of Labor Economics*, 2001.
    Findings: 10-percentage-point increase in immigrant fraction reduces native
    wages by no more than 1 percentage point.
    https://davidcard.berkeley.edu/papers/immig-inflows.pdf

19. **Card, David and Altonji, Joseph.** "The Effects of Immigration on the
    Labor Market Outcomes of Less-skilled Natives." *NBER Working Paper.*
    Findings: 1% immigrant inflow reduces native wages ~1.2%. Modest widening
    of wage gap between skilled/unskilled.
    https://ideas.repec.org/h/nbr/nberch/11773.html

20. **Peri, Giovanni and Ottaviano, Gianmarco.** "Immigration and National
    Wages: Clarifying the Theory and the Empirics." *NBER Working Paper 14188*, 2008.
    Findings: Small negative short-run effects on low-skill workers (-0.7%);
    small positive long-run effects (+0.3%). Imperfect substitution reduces
    competitive effects.
    https://www.nber.org/papers/w14188

21. **Borjas, George J.** "The Labor Demand Curve Is Downward Sloping:
    Reexamining the Impact of Immigration on the Labor Market." *Quarterly
    Journal of Economics*, 2003.
    Findings: Immigration between 1980–2000 reduced average native wages by
    3.2%, with the largest impact on high school dropouts (−7.4%). Argues
    Card/Peri underestimate effects due to geographic diffusion.
    https://scholar.harvard.edu/borjas/publications/labor-demand-curve-downward-sloping

### Quality of life decline and inequality

21. **Pew Research Center.** "How the American Middle Class Has Changed in the
    Past Five Decades." 2022.
    Findings: Middle-class share fell from 61% (1971) to 50% (2021). Lower-income
    tier expanded from 25% to 29%.
    https://www.pewresearch.org/short-reads/2022/04/20/how-the-american-middle-class-has-changed-in-the-past-five-decades/

22. **RAND Corporation (Price, Carter and Edwards, Kathryn).** "Measuring the
    Income Gap from 1975 to 2023." *RAND Working Paper WRA516-2.*
    Findings: $79 trillion cumulative income transferred from the bottom 90% to
    the top since 1975. Bottom 90% earns $3.9 trillion/year less under current
    inequality.
    https://www.rand.org/pubs/working_papers/WRA516-2.html

23. **RAND Corporation.** "Limited Opportunity: Changes in Employment and the
    American Middle Class."
    Findings: Hollowing out of middle-income jobs. Rising education barriers.
    Only half of children outearn parents.
    https://www.rand.org/content/dam/rand/pubs/perspectives/PEA1100/PEA1141-8/RAND_PEA1141-8.pdf

24. **Brookings Institution.** "Seven Reasons to Worry About the American
    Middle Class."
    https://www.brookings.edu/articles/seven-reasons-to-worry-about-the-american-middle-class/

### Immigration, redistricting, and political representation

25. **London School of Economics.** "Identifying Partisan Gerrymandering and
    Its Consequences: Evidence from the 1990 US Census Redistricting." 2025.
    Findings: Democrats packed Hispanic voters into majority-minority districts
    in areas with newly legalized immigrants post-1986 IRCA.
    https://eprints.lse.ac.uk/126163/

26. **Policy Studies Journal.** "The Dynamics of Constituency Representation
    on Immigration Policy in the U.S. House." 2026.
    Findings: Foreign-born populations now influence which party wins seats
    rather than shaping individual legislators' positions.
    https://psjblog.net/2026/01/07/the-dynamics-of-constituency-representation-on-immigration-policy-in-the-u-s-house/

27. **Center for Immigration Studies (Camarota, Steven).** "How Non-Citizens
    Impact Political Representation and the Partisan Makeup of the U.S. House
    of Representatives."
    Findings: 21.6M noncitizens in 2022 census shifted 17 House seats. Gains
    concentrated in Democratic-leaning states.
    https://cis.org/Camarota/How-NonCitizens-Impact-Political-Representation-and-Partisan-Makeup-US-House

28. **Center for Immigration Studies.** "Tilting the Balance: Immigration
    Shifts Congressional Apportionment."
    Findings: Immigration shifted 22 seats including US-born children of
    immigrants in 2020 census.
    https://cis.org/Report/Tilting-Balance

29. **PNAS Nexus.** "Does Enumerating Undocumented Residents in the US Census
    Affect Congressional Apportionment?" 2025.
    https://academic.oup.com/pnasnexus/article/4/2/pgaf021/7985327
